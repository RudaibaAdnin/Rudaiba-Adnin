Revealing Influences of Socioeconomic Factors over Disease Outbreaks

(Published in the ACM COMPASS 2022)

S Mahmudul Hasan, Alabi Mahzabin, Rudaiba Adnin, Ishrat Jahan Eliza, Ishika Tarin, Sadia Afroz, A.B.M. Alim Al Islam

We performed large-scale point-biserial and spearman correlation analysis on the accumulated data to quantify the strength and nature of disease outbreak attributes and socioeconomic indicators relationships. We summarize the nature and prevalent trends between those relationships. We find the primary socioeconomic factors for a disease outbreak exhibiting a particular character. These make our study a novel contribution to the field of disease outbreak analysis and prediction.

Keywords: Socioeconomic factors, Disease outbreaks, Statistical analysis, Correlation

Authors: S Mahmudul Hasan, Alabi Mahzabin, Rudaiba Adnin, Ishrat Jahan Eliza, Ishika Tarin, Sadia Afroz, A.B.M. Alim Al Islam

Keywords: Socioeconomic factors, Disease outbreaks, Statistical analysis, Correlation

The recent Covid-19 pandemic elucidates the need for a better disease outbreak analysis and surveillance system, which can harness state-of-the-art data mining and machine learning techniques to produce better forecasting. In this regard, understanding the correlation between disease outbreaks and socioeconomic factors should pave the way for such systems by providing useful indicators, which are yet to be explored in the literature to the best of our knowledge. Therefore, in this study, we accumulated data on 72 infectious diseases and their outbreaks all over the globe over a period of 23 years as well as corresponding different socioeconomic data. We, then, performed point-biserial and spearman correlation analysis over the collected data. Our analysis of the obtained correlations demonstrates that various disease outbreak attributes are positively and negatively correlated with different socioeconomic indicators. For example, indicators such as lifetime risk of maternal death, adolescent fertility rate, etc., are positively correlated, while indicators such as life expectancy at birth, measles immunization, etc., are negatively correlated, with disease outbreaks that affect the digestive organ system. In this paper, we find and summarize the correlations between 126 outbreak attributes derived from the characteristics of the 72 diseases in consideration and 192 socioeconomic factors which is a novel contribution to the field of disease outbreak analysis and prediction.

"A Tale on Abuse and Its Detection over Online Platforms, Especially over Emails": From the Context of Bangladesh

(Published in 8th International Conference on Networking, Systems, and Security (NSysS 2021))

Ishita Haque, Rudaiba Adnin, Sadia Afroz, Faria Huq, Sazan Mahbub, A. B. M. Alim Al Islam

We perform a mixed-method user study consisting of formative interviews (n=15) and a survey (n=65) of users' experiences related to online abuse, especially focusing on abuse over emails. Our findings also highlight a certain level of user preference for an automated abuse detection system potentially considering its more control and fewer privacy concerns to users. These findings point to opportunities for design interventions for hybrid abuse detection (automated and human-moderation-based) systems, which is the most preferred system to the users.

Keywords: Online abuse, Abuse detection systems, Privacy, User studies

Citadel: An Automated Abuse Detection System to Detect and Prevent Abusive Behaviors over Emails

Published in 8th International Conference on Networking, Systems, and Security (NSysS 2021)

Ishita Haque, Rudaiba Adnin, Sadia Afroz, Faria Huq, Sazan Mahbub, A. B. M.Alim Al Islam

We iteratively develop an automated abuse detection system "Citadel" for emails in two sequential phases and evaluate in both the phases - first over 39 participants through in-person demonstrations, and second over 21 participants through a 3-day field study and over 63 participants through a video demonstration. Evaluation results portray efficacy, efficiency, and user acceptance of "Citadel" in detecting and preventing abusive emails.

Keywords: Abuse detection system, Privacy, Human-centered computing, Emails, User studies

My Role: Problem Formulation, System Impelementation, Evaluation, Manuscript Preparation, Revision

Students' Adoption of Online Platforms for Learning Purposes in Bangladesh

Published in HCI International 2022

Rudaiba Adnin, Sadia Afroz, Montaser Majid Taseen, Sadia Sharmin

We perform a mixed-method study through semi-structured interviews with 18 university students and an online survey of 239 university students from an engineering university situated in Bangladesh. Our findings reveal several influential factors, experiences, and challenges of the conventional usage of online platforms for learning. One of the noteworthy findings of our study is that most students use multiple online platforms for an effective learning experience.

Focusing on the Unfocused: Investigating Online Interactions with Small-Scale Non-Profit Organizations Working for Street Children in Bangladesh

(Under Review in the International Journal of Human-computer Studies)

Rudaiba Adnin, Ishita Haque, Sadia Afroz, Alvi Md. Ishmam, Sriram Chelappan, A.B.M. Alim Al Islam

To investigate the challenges and their workarounds to address those challenges, we interview seven NPOs working for street children and 21 current donors, and survey 42 potential donors in a developing country (Bangladesh). Our findings reveal influential factors pertinent to the non-profit work and gaps in interactions among the stakeholders (small-scale NPOs, donors, and potential donors) while using existing online platforms. We provide several design implications for enabling better connectivity among the stakeholders that focus on their essential role, reducing their challenges, and resource constraints.

Keywords: Non-profit organizations, Donors, Online platforms, Interview

My Role: Corresponding Author, Manuscript Preparation, Interview, Revision

EmoBot: Artificial Emotion Generation through an Emotional Chatbot during General-purpose Conversation

(Under Review in the International Journal of Human-computer Studies)

Md Ehtesham-Ul-Haque, Jacob D'Rozario, Rudaiba Adnin, Farhan Tanvir Utshaw, Fabiha Tasneem, A.B.M. Alim Al Islam

Based on the Cognitive Appraisal Theory, and focusing on audio and textual inputs, we propose a novel method to calculate informative variables to evaluate a particular emotion-generating event and six primary emotions. We incorporate these mappings into an audio chatbot, namely, EmoBot. An objective evaluation indicates that EmoBot could generate more accurate emotional and semantic responses than the traditional chatbots that do not consider emotion. Additionally, a subjective evaluation of EmoBot demonstrates its effectiveness to improve user satisfaction over a traditional chatbot that does not consider emotion.

Keywords: Emotion generation, Psychological Theory, User study

My Role: Corresponding Author, Manuscript Preparation (Problem Formulation, Contribution, Background, Introduction, Discussion), Revision
